Environmental

Microsoft has made significant progress in its environmental sustainability journey, marking 2025 as the midpoint toward becoming a carbon negative, water positive, and zero waste company. The company increased its renewable energy procurement substantially, from 1.8 gigawatts in 2020 to 34 gigawatts in 2024, and contracted nearly 30 million metric tons of carbon removal, contributing to scaling the carbon removal market. It has provided clean water and sanitation to over 1.5 million people and plans to replenish more than 100 million cubic meters of water globally. Progress toward zero waste includes new Circular Centers that enable reuse and recycling of nearly 91% of servers and components decommissioned from datacenters, and product packaging recyclability has reached nearly 95%. These achievements reflect ongoing efforts rather than completed targets, with 2025 serving as a milestone year in a longer-term commitment .
Social

Microsoft demonstrates a longstanding commitment to diversity, inclusion, and human rights. Employee engagement surveys show that 83.5% of employees observed intentional allyship actions, and 81.2% agreed that Microsoft fosters a diverse and inclusive environment. Representation of women increased by 0.4 percentage points year over year, Hispanic and Latinx employees by 0.1 percentage point, and global women in technical roles by 0.5 percentage points. Representation of employees with disabilities also increased, while Black and African American representation saw a slight decrease. Indigenous employee data is now shared for the first time .

Microsoft's human rights due diligence (HRDD) processes are robust and aligned with international standards such as the UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights. The company conducts regular risk assessments, including human rights impact assessments at enterprise and regional levels, and engages with external stakeholders and experts. Transparency is maintained through public reporting and independent scrutiny, including participation in the Global Network Initiative (GNI), where Microsoft undergoes comprehensive independent assessments to ensure compliance and continuous improvement .
Governance

Microsoft's Board of Directors maintains strong oversight of environmental, social, and governance (ESG) matters, including cybersecurity and AI strategy risks. The Environmental, Social, and Public Policy Committee provides guidance on responsible AI policies and programs, receiving regular updates from management. The Board's committees meet regularly, with the Audit Committee holding 8 meetings, the Compensation Committee 5, the Environmental, Social, and Public Policy Committee 4, and the Governance and Nominating Committee 4 in fiscal year 2025 .

Executive compensation is designed to align with long-term strategic goals and shareholder interests. For fiscal year 2025, Named Executives had target cash incentives and equity awards tied to rigorous performance metrics, with CEO Satya Nadella's compensation being over 95% performance-based. Financial performance measures resulted in a weighted payout of 117% of target, reflecting strong company performance .

Microsoft commits to responsible AI development and deployment, with governance frameworks to uphold principles consistently across the company. The Board maintains direct oversight over AI governance and regulation risks, ensuring alignment with customer expectations and regulatory requirements .

Shareholder rights include annual election of directors, majority voting standards, proxy access bylaws, and significant stock ownership requirements for directors and executives. The Board emphasizes independence, diversity, and ongoing evaluation of governance practices .
Key Risks and Data Gaps

Despite progress, challenges remain in data transparency and environmental disclosure. The environmental impact of AI workloads is difficult to quantify precisely due to lack of distinction between AI and non-AI workloads in data center reporting and insufficient granular data from data center operators. Embedded water consumption in purchased electricity is rarely disclosed, limiting accuracy in assessing water footprints. Additionally, some employee demographic groups have seen slight decreases in representation, indicating ongoing diversity challenges .
Overall Assessment

Microsoft reports substantial progress toward its environmental sustainability goals, with clear interim achievements in renewable energy procurement, carbon removal, water replenishment, and waste reduction. Socially, the company maintains a strong focus on diversity, inclusion, and human rights due diligence, supported by transparent reporting and independent assessments. Governance structures provide comprehensive oversight of ESG risks, executive compensation aligned with performance, and responsible AI governance.
